WeWave is a mobile and desktop platform that provides the world's first draw to search engines for stocks and cryptos

Introduction

WeWave is a fast-growing investment platform that simplifies market analysis for stocks and cryptocurrencies. With its intuitive platform, traders can make informed investment decisions, stay on top of market trends, and discover new opportunities.

We developed the WeWave platform from the ground up and launched it as an MVP within a few months (June 2021). Then we added more features as the official launch took place in November 2021.


My Role

In my role as the UI/UX designer for WeWave, I was responsible for leading the design of both the mobile and desktop web versions of the product. I worked with a team of product designers, product managers and financial advisors, and engineers to uncover insights and translate concepts into features that address user needs.

Define

What's the problem?

Stock trading has become increasingly popular in recent years with the rise of online trading platforms. However, traders often struggle to understand the stock market and find new stock ideas to add to their portfolios due to usability issues in existing platforms. This leads to frustration and a lack of confidence in their abilities to make informed investment decisions.


Vision

Designing for what users want to use

Our vision was to create an investing platform that makes it easy and simple to observe, analyze, and interpret data on stocks and cryptocurrencies in one user-friendly environment that allowed users to save time, get more trading ideas, have no coding required, and be a beginner-friendly platform. With the WeWave database, we provide fast and accurate data analysis for our users.

Reflection

What I learned from this

I take great pride in having contributed to a large-scale project right from its inception. I extend my gratitude to my team for their unwavering support and trust in me throughout the process. This being my first UI/UX project, working alongside cross-functional teams such as engineers, project managers, and the marketing team, and delivering the final output, was a tremendous learning experience for me. I challenged myself to think of numerous iterations, presented them to the teams, and tested them with users. It is heartening to see that WeWave has now been launched as a live product, with over 10,000 registered users. Moving forward, I plan to devote more time to responsive web design for future enhancements.
